Japanese Milk Bread

Prep Time

20 mins

Total Time

3 hours

Yield

4 loafs

Ingredients

Tangzhong
  • 4 tbsp (40g) bread flour
  • 2 tbsp (48g) water
  • 8 tbsp (120g) whole milk
Bread Dough:
  • 5 cups (640g) bread flour
  • 2.5 cups (320g) bread flour
  • 2 Tbsp (18g) active dry yeast (instant works as well)
  • 0.5 tbsp (6g) fine sea salt
  • 1 cup (240g) whole milk
  • 1/2 cup (112g) granulated sugar
  • 6 Tbsp (84g) unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 whole egg, room temperature
Egg Wash:
  • 1 egg
  • Splash of whole milk (2tbsp)

Instructions

Milk Bread:

1. For the tangzhong, add all ingredients together and mix over medium heat, and constantly whisk until it becomes a paste.

2. Once thick, remove from heat and let it cool to room temperature.

3. In a stand mixer bowl, add the dry ingredients. Add in milk and yeast mixture, the tangzhong mix, and a whole egg. Mix on low speed, scraping the sides occasionally. When dough starts coming together, increase speed to medium-low. Gradually add butter until incorporated and nothing sticks to the sides. About 5-7 minutes.

4.Dump dough onto a work surface and roll into a tight ball. Place dough into a lightly greased bowl and cover with a damp towel or plastic wrap. Let rise at room temperature for 1-2 hours, or doubled in size.

5.Roll out the dough into an oval shape. Flip it over so the surface (crust) is on the bottom. Letter fold: Fold the top and bottom third over the center. Rotate 90Â° and roll it up to form a spiral while pressing down. Pinch the seam closed and add three pieces seam side down into a loaf pan. Proof in a warm, humid place until it doubles in size or it's 1cm below the rim of the loaf pan (1-2hours)

6. Preheat oven to 350 F and bake for 30 minutes or until internal temp is 190 F
